# Use Cinatra for Drupal

Once the module is connected and your role has access (see the
[quick start](./quick-start.md)), the assistant is available while you work.

## Where the assistant appears

The Cinatra assistant panel shows up on:

- **Node pages** — while viewing a piece of content.
- **Node edit forms** — while creating or editing content.
- **The front page** — as a general entry point.

The panel is a chat surface next to your content. It is only visible to users in
the roles you granted access to.

## What the assistant knows

The assistant is aware of the page you are on, so its suggestions fit the content
you are actually editing. You do not have to paste your text in — describe what
you want and the assistant works from the context of the current page.

## Typical things you do

- **Draft from scratch.** Ask for an introduction, a section, or a summary, and
  the assistant produces a draft you can refine.
- **Rewrite and tighten.** Ask it to shorten, expand, simplify, or change the
  tone of existing text.
- **Fix and polish.** Ask it to correct wording, improve a headline, or suggest
  metadata.

## You stay in control

The editor always reviews what the assistant suggests and decides what to keep.
Nothing changes your content automatically. When your Cinatra instance supports
it, an accepted suggestion can be dropped straight into the form field you are
editing; otherwise you copy the parts you want. Either way, the decision is
yours.
